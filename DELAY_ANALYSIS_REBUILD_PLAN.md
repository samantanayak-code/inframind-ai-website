## Executive Summary Strategy

**Single sentence positioning:** "Delay analysis built by the practitioner who administers FIDIC contracts, deployed on India's largest high-speed rail megaproject, and tested in ICC arbitration."

**Executive summary arc:**
1. **Open with the financial stake** — "Every day of unsubstantiated delay on an EPC megaproject represents LD exposure, contractual risk, and weakened dispute position."
2. **Name the consequence** — "Under FIDIC Sub-Clause 20.1, a single missed notice deadline can forfeit millions in EOT entitlement."
3. **Present the differentiator** — "InfraMind EPC delivers delay analysis and EOT preparation grounded in 20+ years of hands-on contract administration — not academic theory."
4. **Quantify the value** — Table: EOT recovery (60-90%), LD reduction (50-80%), notice compliance (100%)
5. **Close with the credential** — "ICC Arbitration Fact Witness. Live deployment on MAHSR T-3 (INR 3,142 Crore)."

## Writing Standards Applied

| Standard | Rule |
|----------|------|
| Audience | EPC clients, project directors, legal/commercial leadership |
| Tone | Executive, confident, evidence-based |
| Sentence length | Max 25 words. Average 15-18 words. |
| AI pattern elimination | No "In today's rapidly evolving landscape." No "Let's delve into." No "It is worth noting that." No "In conclusion." |
| Active voice | "We deliver" not "Services are delivered by us." |
| Scannability | Every section readable in 30 seconds. Key takeaway visible within 2 seconds. |
| Claims | Every claim paired with evidence or context. |
| First page impact | First paragraph establishes stakes, differentiator, and credential. |
